Understanding Circadian Rhythms

The master clock that governs your sleep-wake cycle

What Are Circadian Rhythms?

Circadian rhythms are physical, mental, and behavioral changes that follow a 24-hour cycle. These natural processes respond primarily to light and darkness and affect most living things, including animals, plants, and microbes.

The Science Behind Your Body Clock

The Suprachiasmatic Nucleus (SCN)

Located in the hypothalamus, the SCN is your master clock. It contains about 20,000 neurons that synchronize your body's rhythms by:

  • Responding to light signals from the eyes
  • Controlling melatonin production
  • Regulating body temperature
  • Influencing hormone release

Key Hormones

Melatonin: The sleep hormone, produced by the pineal gland when darkness falls. Levels rise around 9 PM and peak in the middle of the night.

Cortisol: The stress/alertness hormone. Levels peak in the early morning (6-8 AM) to help you wake up, then gradually decline throughout the day.

Circadian Disruption

Modern life often conflicts with our natural rhythms:

  • Blue light exposure: Screens suppress melatonin production
  • Irregular schedules: Shift work and social jetlag
  • Indoor living: Insufficient bright light during the day
  • Late meals: Eating cues conflicting with sleep cues

How nuyu Supports Natural Rhythms

The nuyu device works with your circadian biology:

  • Light timing: Warm evening light, bright morning light
  • Consistent cues: Same-time signals every day
  • Multi-sensory: Scent adds another zeitgeber
  • Gradual transitions: Mimics natural dawn and dusk

Chronotype Consideration

Your chronotype (whether you're a morning lark or night owl) is partly genetic. The nuyu method works with your natural type, not against it.
