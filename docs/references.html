Scientific References

Peer-reviewed research supporting the nuyu method

Sleep and Circadian Rhythms

Czeisler, C. A., et al. (1989). "Bright light induction of strong (type 0) resetting of the human circadian pacemaker." Science, 244(4910), 1328-1333.

Dijk, D. J., & Lockley, S. W. (2002). "Integration of human sleep-wake regulation and circadian rhythmicity." Journal of Applied Physiology, 92(2), 852-862.

Roenneberg, T., et al. (2003). "Life between clocks: daily temporal patterns of human chronotypes." Journal of Biological Rhythms, 18(1), 80-90.

Scent and Sleep

Goel, N., Kim, H., & Lao, R. P. (2005). "An olfactory stimulus modifies nighttime sleep in young men and women." Chronobiology International, 22(5), 889-904.

Rihm, J. S., et al. (2014). "Reactivating memories during sleep by odors: odor specificity and associated changes in sleep oscillations." Journal of Cognitive Neuroscience, 26(8), 1806-1818.

Rasch, B., et al. (2007). "Odor cues during slow-wave sleep prompt declarative memory consolidation." Science, 315(5817), 1426-1429.

Habit Formation

Clear, J. (2018). Atomic Habits: An Easy & Proven Way to Build Good Habits & Break Bad Ones. New York: Avery.

Duhigg, C. (2012). The Power of Habit: Why We Do What We Do in Life and Business. New York: Random House.

Wood, W., & Rünger, D. (2016). "Psychology of habit." Annual Review of Psychology, 67, 289-314.

Lally, P., et al. (2010). "How are habits formed: Modelling habit formation in the real world." European Journal of Social Psychology, 40(6), 998-1009.

Light and Melatonin

Brainard, G. C., et al. (2001). "Action spectrum for melatonin regulation in humans: evidence for a novel circadian photoreceptor." Journal of Neuroscience, 21(16), 6405-6412.

Cajochen, C., et al. (2003). "High sensitivity of human melatonin, alertness, thermoregulation, and heart rate to short wavelength light." The Journal of Clinical Endocrinology & Metabolism, 90(3), 1311-1316.

Sleep and Performance

Walker, M. (2017). Why We Sleep: Unlocking the Power of Sleep and Dreams. New York: Scribner.

Dinges, D. F., et al. (1997). "Cumulative sleepiness, mood disturbance, and psychomotor vigilance performance decrements during a week of sleep restricted to 4-5 hours per night." Sleep, 20(4), 267-277.
